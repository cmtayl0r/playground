More than once I've seen a dog

waiting for its owner outside a café

practically implode with worry

Oh, God, what if she doesn't come back this time? What will I do?

Who will take care of me?

I loved her so much and now she's gone

I'm tied to a post

surrounded by people who don't look or smell or sound like her at all

And when she does come

what a flurry of commotion

What a chorus of yelping and cooing

It's almost unbearable

This sudden fullness

after such total loss

The world made whole again

by a hand on the shoulder and a voice like no other
